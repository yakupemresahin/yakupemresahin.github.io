Yakup Emre Şahin

I’m a PhD researcher in Deep Learning at the University of Liège, working on scientific foundation models at the intersection of machine learning and physics. Previously I worked with Helmholtz Munich (Helmholtz AI), CERN’s Beam Instrumentation Group, and the Gravity Group at Koç University. I hold an M.Sc. in Physics (AI) from LMU Munich and a double B.Sc. in Electrical & Electronics Engineering and Physics from Boğaziçi University. My work has appeared in Physical Review D and CERN conference proceedings; I’m a recipient of the DAAD–TEV Master’s Scholarship.

Research

I'm interested in computer vision, deep learning, generative AI, and image processing. Most of my research is about inferring the physical world (shape, motion, color, light, etc) from images, usually with radiance fields. Some papers are highlighted.
